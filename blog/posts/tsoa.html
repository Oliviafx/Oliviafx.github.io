The Song of Achilles: This Broke My Heart & I Love It

December 1, 2020

I had a friend recommend this to me with the caveat that it was an adorable romance up until everyone died. Which, knowing the myth of Achilles and Patroclus, wasn't exactly a spoiler but did make me shy away from the story as someone who loves happily-ever-afters in fiction (we have enough grimdark in the real world right now). But I finally picked it up and I don't regret it at all.

This story was absolutely amazing. Told from Patroclus' point of view, we get a first-hand seat of the beautiful Achilles, whose half-immortal parentage immediately helps us figure out why Patroclus is almost bestotted from the start (and Achilles' mother is immenesely scary). I didn't stop crying throughout the whole last thirty something pages, this book wrecking me in the absolute best way possible.

"He is half of my soul, as the poets say."

I read a blog post by Maggie Stiefvater that talked about books that fed her - books that inspired her as a writer to write. And I'm sure that this one will be one for me. The absolutely gorgeous prose that Miller uses throughout has given me so many lines that completely render me speechless every time, the way that she manages to craft this story together and show a humane side of Achilles, the arrogant solider, allowed me to completely embrace this story.

Title: The Song of Achilles

Author: Madeline Miller

Greece in the age of heroes. Patroclus, an awkward young prince, has been exiled to the court of King Peleus and his perfect son Achilles. By all rights their paths should never cross, but Achilles takes the shamed prince as his friend, and as they grow into young men skilled in the arts of war and medicine their bond blossoms into something deeper - despite the displeasure of Achilles' mother Thetis, a cruel sea goddess. But then word comes that Helen of Sparta has been kidnapped. Torn between love and fear for his friend, Patroclus journeys with Achilles to Troy, little knowing that the years that follow will test everything they hold dear.
